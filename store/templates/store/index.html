What is Elbow Bump Apparel?

In the Autumn of 2020, in the brief interlude between countless London Lockdowns, we decided that we wanted to come out of this crazy period with something to show for it, and something to look back on with pride.


In a West London pub, Elbow Bump was born. As ideas kept coming to us, our ambition to make something different to every other clothing brand you see on your Instagram feed grew too. The premise behind Elbow Bump is that every decision we make is done with an emphasis on choosing the most sustainable option that is feasible. For example, we only use organic cotton in our t-shirts, and our hoodies are made of a blend of organic cotton and recycled polyester. All our designs are made with water based transfers, without chemicals, and our mailing bags are made from sugarcane grown on reclaimed wasteland. Even our sticky tape is completely biodegradable!


Nothing turns us off more than "fast fashion" and therefore, when you buy something from Elbow Bump, you can rest assured that you are part of the solution.

It's time to Cotton On!

Organic cotton uses 91% less water to produce and emits 46% less greenhouse gasses than non-organic. That means choosing to buy an organic cotton tee can save 2,457 litres of water - enough for one person to drink 8 glasses of water daily for 3 and a half years! Cotton accounts for 16% of the worlds insecticides, 7% of the worlds pesticides and 69% of the water footprint generated by the textiles industry! Currently, less than 1% of cotton grown worldwide is organic.

What else are we doing to lead by example?

Globally, more than 1 trillion single-use plastic bags are used each year, and less than 5% get recycled. We use mailing bags made of sugarcane grown on reclaimed wasteland, making it 100% biodegradable and recyclable. Even our sticky tape and mailing cards are 100% biodegradable!

Nearly half a trillion plastic water bottles are purchased and consumed each year, and fewer than 7% are recycled into new water bottles. Our hoodies are made up of 30% recycled polyester, made from recycled water bottles.
